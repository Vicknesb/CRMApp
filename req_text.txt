1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose
This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document defines the functional and non-functional requirements for a custom Customer Relationship Management (CRM) application tailored for an IT service company. It serves as the primary reference for developers, designers, testers, and stakeholders involved in the design, development, and deployment of the system.
1.2 Scope
The CRM application will support end-to-end management of customer relationships for an IT service organization. The system will cover the following key areas:
Lead capture and qualification from multiple channels
Client account and contact management
IT service opportunity and deal tracking
Project and service delivery management
Support ticket and SLA management
Communication and activity logging
Invoicing and contract management
Analytics, reporting, and dashboards
Integration with third-party IT tools (email, Jira, Slack, etc.)

2. Overall Description
2.1 Product Perspective
The CRM application will be a web-based, cloud-hosted platform accessible via modern browsers and mobile devices. It will act as the central hub for all customer-facing and internal sales operations of the IT service company, integrating with email clients, project management tools, accounting software, and communication platforms.

2.3 Operating Environment
Platform: Cloud-hosted (AWS / Azure / GCP)
Frontend: Web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari) + responsive mobile view
Backend: RESTful API with microservices architecture
Database: Relational (PostgreSQL) + optional NoSQL for logs/analytics
Authentication: OAuth 2.0 / SSO with 2FA support
Deployment: Docker containers via CI/CD pipeline
2.4 Assumptions & Constraints
The company already uses Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace for email
Initial deployment will support up to 200 concurrent users
All data must reside within India (data residency requirement)
The system must comply with ISO/IEC 27001 security standards
Mobile app (iOS/Android) is a Phase 2 deliverable
3. Functional Requirements
3.1 Lead Management
3.1.1 Lead Capture
Capture leads from web forms, email, LinkedIn, referrals, and manual entry
Auto-assign leads to sales reps based on territory, service type, or round-robin rules
Detect and merge duplicate leads automatically
Capture UTM parameters and lead source tracking
3.1.2 Lead Qualification
Lead scoring engine based on configurable criteria (company size, industry, budget, engagement)
Categorize leads as MQL, SQL, or Unqualified
Support BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline) qualification framework
Visual Kanban board to move leads through qualification stages
3.1.3 Lead Conversion
One-click conversion of qualified lead to Account, Contact, and Opportunity
Preserve full history and activities during conversion
3.2 Contact & Account Management
3.2.1 Contact Records
Store contact details: name, designation, email, phone, LinkedIn, timezone
Associate multiple contacts to a single account
Tag contacts with roles: Decision Maker, Influencer, End User, Technical POC
Log interaction history, emails, calls, and notes per contact
3.2.2 Account (Company) Records
Maintain company profile: name, industry, size, location, website, annual revenue
Track account health score based on activity and engagement
Support parent-child account hierarchy (e.g., HQ and subsidiaries)
Associate all related contacts, deals, tickets, and documents to the account
Define account tier: Strategic, Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB
3.3 Opportunity & Sales Pipeline
3.3.1 Deal Management
Create opportunities with fields: name, value, expected close date, service type, stage
Link opportunities to contacts and accounts
Support multiple deal pipelines (e.g., New Business, Renewals, Upsell)
Track probability of winning per stage
3.3.2 Pipeline View
Interactive Kanban and list views for deal stages
Drag-and-drop to move deals across stages
Color-code deals by value, age, or priority
Revenue forecasting based on weighted pipeline value
3.3.3 Proposal & Quote Management
Generate service proposals with itemized pricing from product/service catalog
Support approval workflows for quotes above a threshold
Send proposals via email directly from the CRM with tracking (opened/viewed)
Version control for proposal revisions
3.4 Activity & Task Management
Log calls, emails, meetings, demos, and site visits against contacts/accounts/deals
Create tasks and reminders with due dates, priorities, and assignees
Calendar view showing all scheduled activities
Auto-log emails from connected mailbox (Gmail / Outlook)
Meeting scheduler with calendar integration and video conferencing link generation
Activity completion tracking and overdue alerts
3.5 Service & Support Ticketing
3.5.1 Ticket Management
Create tickets from email, web portal, phone, or manual entry
Categorize tickets: Incident, Service Request, Change Request, Problem
Assign priority levels: Critical, High, Medium, Low
Link tickets to accounts, contacts, and projects
Internal notes and customer-visible updates on each ticket
3.5.2 SLA Management
Define SLA policies by ticket priority and client tier
Track response time and resolution time against SLA targets
Automated escalation when SLA breach is approaching
SLA compliance reports per client and per team
3.5.3 Knowledge Base
Create and maintain internal KB articles for common IT issues
Suggest relevant KB articles when creating a ticket
Track article usage and helpfulness ratings
3.6 Project & Delivery Management
Create IT projects linked to deals/accounts with scope, timeline, and budget
Manage project phases and milestones with target dates
Assign team members to tasks with effort estimates
Track project status: Not Started, In Progress, On Hold, Completed, Delayed
Integrate with Jira for project task sync (bidirectional)
Document management: attach SOWs, technical specs, and deliverables
Generate project status reports for clients
3.7 Contract & Renewal Management
Manage service contracts with start/end dates, value, terms, and auto-renewal flag
Associate contracts to accounts and deals
Automated renewal reminders at 90, 60, and 30 days before expiry
Contract version history and digital signature support
Track contract amendments and change orders
3.8 Invoicing & Revenue Tracking
Generate invoices from deals and contracts
Track payment status: Draft, Sent, Paid, Overdue, Cancelled
Integrate with accounting tools (Zoho Books, QuickBooks, Tally)
Revenue recognition reporting by month/quarter/year
Multi-currency support with exchange rate management
3.9 Marketing & Campaign Management
Create and manage email marketing campaigns with segmented audience lists
Track campaign performance: open rate, click rate, conversion rate
Drip email sequences for lead nurturing
Event management: webinars, tech talks, and conferences
Campaign ROI tracking — link campaigns to resulting deals
3.10 Communication & Collaboration
Unified communication log per contact/account (email, call, chat, meeting)
In-app notifications and @mention for team collaboration
Slack / Microsoft Teams integration for deal and ticket alerts
Email templates for sales, support, and onboarding
Internal chat / comment threads on records

5. Non-Functional Requirements
5.1 Performance
Page load time: under 2 seconds for all primary views on standard broadband
API response time: 95th percentile under 500ms
Support 200 concurrent users without performance degradation
Bulk operations (import/export of 10,000+ records) must complete within 5 minutes
5.2 Security
Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) with field-level and record-level permissions
Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) via OTP or authenticator app
All data encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.3)
Audit logs for all data modifications, login events, and admin actions
OWASP Top 10 vulnerability mitigation
Penetration testing before go-live and annually thereafter
Session timeout after 30 minutes of inactivity
5.3 Reliability & Availability
System uptime SLA: 99.9% (excluding planned maintenance)
Automated daily database backups with 30-day retention
Disaster recovery RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour
Health monitoring with automated alerting on service failures
5.4 Scalability
Architecture must support horizontal scaling to handle user growth up to 1,000 users
Database designed to handle up to 10 million records without performance loss
Microservices design to allow independent scaling of high-load modules
5.5 Usability
Responsive design supporting desktop, tablet, and mobile browsers
Consistent UI/UX following WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards
Onboarding wizard for new users with contextual help tooltips
Maximum 3 clicks to reach any core function from the home dashboard
5.6 Maintainability
Codebase must follow clean architecture with at least 80% unit test coverage
API-first design with Swagger/OpenAPI documentation
Feature flags for controlled rollout of new functionality
Centralized logging (ELK stack or equivalent) for debugging

7. UI / UX Requirements
7.1 General Design Principles
Clean, minimal interface inspired by modern SaaS tools (Notion, Linear, HubSpot)
Dark mode and light mode support with user preference saved per account
Consistent color-coding: green for won/active, red for critical/lost, amber for at-risk
Collapsible sidebar navigation with role-based menu visibility
7.2 Key Screens
Home Dashboard — personalized widgets: My Tasks, Pipeline Summary, Recent Activities, KPIs
Lead Board — Kanban with drag-and-drop columns per qualification stage
Account 360 View — full account profile with all associated contacts, deals, tickets, contracts
Deal Detail — timeline view of all activities, documents, notes, and stage history
Ticket Queue — filterable list with SLA countdown timers and priority flags
Analytics Dashboard — role-specific pre-built reports with drill-down capability
Admin Panel — user management, workflow builder, field configurator, audit logs
7.3 Mobile Requirements (Phase 1 — Responsive Web)
Fully responsive layouts for screens 375px and wider
Core actions accessible on mobile: create/view records, log activity, update ticket status
Push notifications via browser for task reminders and SLA alerts
8. Data Requirements
8.1 Data Migration
Support import of contacts, accounts, and deals from CSV / Excel
Migration wizard with field mapping, validation, and error reporting
Support one-time migration from legacy tools (Salesforce, Zoho, HubSpot) via API
8.2 Data Retention & Archiving
Active records retained indefinitely while account is active
Deleted records moved to recycle bin for 30-day recovery window
Archived accounts retained for 7 years to meet compliance requirements
Automated data archiving for records older than 5 years
8.3 Data Compliance
Full GDPR / PDPB (India Personal Data Protection Bill) compliance
Data export on request (right to portability)
Ability to anonymize or delete individual records on request (right to erasure)
Consent management for marketing communication opt-ins

11. Acceptance Criteria
11.1 Functional Acceptance
All Must Have requirements in Section 3 implemented and tested
Zero Critical or High severity bugs in production release
All third-party integrations (email, calendar, Jira) verified end-to-end
Data migration completed with less than 0.1% error rate
11.2 Performance Acceptance
Load test confirms system handles 200 concurrent users with page load < 2 seconds
API response time P95 < 500ms under load
11.3 Security Acceptance
Penetration test passed with no Critical or High vulnerabilities
2FA, RBAC, and audit logging functional for all roles
Data encryption verified at rest and in transit
11.4 UAT Sign-Off
Signed UAT approval from Sales Lead, Support Lead, and IT Manager
Training documentation and video walkthroughs delivered
Helpdesk and runbook documentation handed over to the operations team
